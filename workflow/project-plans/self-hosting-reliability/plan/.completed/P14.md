## Implemented

### `V1Adapter::build_instance` — `src/engine/recovery/adapters/v1.rs`

Reconstructs a `WorkflowInstance` from the immutable capsule bytes by
canonical deserialization:

- `serde_json::from_slice::<WorkflowType>(&capsule.resolved_workflow_bytes)`
- `serde_json::from_slice::<WorkflowConfig>(&capsule.resolved_config_bytes)`
- `WorkflowInstance::create_with_run_id(workflow, config, &capsule.run_id)`

The instance carries the capsule's exact `run_id` (the capsule authority),
starts at the workflow's first step, and is transitioned to the reserved step
by the executor before execution. No synthetic reconstruction; the exact
canonical bytes persisted at launch are deserialized.

### `RunnerRecoveryExecutor::build_resume_runner` — `src/engine/recovery/wiring.rs`

Production capsule-backed execution:

1. `adapter_for(invocation.capsule)` → `Box<dyn CapsuleAdapter>` (object-safe
   dispatch, fail-closed for unknown versions).
2. `adapter.build_instance(invocation.capsule)` → `WorkflowInstance`.
3. `instance.transition_to(invocation.step_id)` — starts at the reserved step
   without reopening or advancing the recovery epoch.
4. `EngineRunner::with_db_path_and_context(instance, ExecutorRegistry::with_defaults(), &self.db_path, self.run_context)` —
   existing resume construction pattern; loads checkpoint state, builds step
   context, operates on the executor's own connection outside the protocol's
   SQLite writer transaction.
5. `runner.execute_step(invocation.step_id)` — real runner behavior; the
   actual `StepOutcome` is returned.
6. `map_outcome_status(&outcome)` — maps the actual outcome truthfully:
   - `Success` → `step_status = "completed"`
   - `Wait` → `step_status = "interrupted"` (resumable)
   - any other outcome → `step_status = "failed"`
   No synthetic success is fabricated.
